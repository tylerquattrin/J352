Meet Emma Levin

UMD Journalism Major

Emma Levine is a sophomore journalism major with a public leadership minor at the University of Maryland. She’s originally from Flemington, New Jersey.

Her interest in journalism began when she started freelancing for her local newspaper. There, she experienced being censored.

“That inspired me to keep writing even when people don't like what you have to say,” Levine said. “It came across as this industry is a challenge, and that showed me I was up for that.”

If Levine could choose one superpower, it would be teleportation, since her journalism work requires her to be at many different places.

“I have to be a lot of places, and it would just make my life easier,” she said.

Among those places is the Xfinity Center, where she works for Maryland Athletics, creating videos for the Terps.

Over winter break, Levine traveled to Indian Shores, Florida, staying in an Airbnb for six days. She spent her time there on the beach, going for walks and eating lots of seafood. She ate up to eight lobster rolls, she said.

Levine said there’s really no food she despises, but she doesn’t enjoy mushrooms.
